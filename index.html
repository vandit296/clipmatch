    How it works: each audio clip's waveform is slid across the video's audio track
    (normalized cross-correlation) to find the exact matching moment — accurate to milliseconds,
    even if the audio and video recordings started at different times or have different
    loudness/EQ. This prototype reports timestamps and generates the cut commands;
    a desktop version renders the video clips itself.
